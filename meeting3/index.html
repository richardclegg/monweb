Mathematics of Networks 3rd Meeting (20th December 2004) --- Christmas Special

The meeting took place on 20th December at Imperial College London. The location is Studio A in the Huxley Building. Slides from the talks are available below. Thanks to Uli Harder at Imperial for playing host for this meeting.

This meeting is part of a series of meetings on this topic --- there is also a mailing list mainly for information about these meetings.

    Schedule

    11:00-11:30Arrival and coffee
    11:30-12:15Yann Golanski (University of York) "On Simple Routing Games"(Abstract) (Slides)
    12:15-1:00 Ashok Argent-Katwala (Imperial College) "Peer-To-Peer Networks" (Abstract) (Slides)
    1:00-2:00 Lunch
    2:00-2:45Min Chen (BT/University of York) "Random walks in networks" (Abstract) (Slides)
    2:45-3:30 Raul Mondragon (Queen Mary,University of London) "Centrality and Congestion in Data" (Abstract) (Slides)
    3:30-4:00Coffee Break
    4:00-4:45 Damon Wischik (University College London) "Queueing Theory and TCP Congestion control" (Abstract)(Further Work)
    4:45-5:30 Fabrice Saffre (BT) "Internet Wormholes. Can security patches travel faster than light?" (Abstract)
    5:30-6:00 Closing Discussion
    6:00End of conference and drinks at nearby hostelry
