Mathematics of Networks (Clegg Abstract)

Long-range dependence (a statistical phenomenon related to the autocorrelation function) in the internet is a subject which has had a lot of research interest over the last ten years. In the literature, four causal mechanisms are suggested:

  • Data traffic may be generated in a way which is LRD as it leaves the computer (e.g. VBR video traffic).
  • LRD may arise from the heavy tail of the sizes of transferred files.
  • LRD may arise from feedback mechanisms in the TCP protocol.
  • LRD may arise from the topology of the network itself.

This talk attempts to explain the four mechanisms and provide some insight into which are genuinely of importance.
