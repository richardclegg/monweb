Mathematics of Networks (Smith Abstract)

I have been doing some work on networks with "variable" demand and maybe some of that might be of interest in telecoms network modelling. One of the models used in transportation modelling is a kind of gravity model (the doubly constrained gravity model). This allows demand for certain source-sink pairs to grow because the deterrence is small for those pairs.

I would describe that (and other demand models), and the often currently assumed "equilibrium" interaction between supply and demand for transport, as something that might be relevant to telecoms. I would also consider the problem of using controls optimally in the face of a variable demand model.
